Exposition:

This song is mostly about a former relationship, in particular her difficulties with trust, and how that made it difficult to trust her in turn.
The first two verses are mainly about a particular moment where she looked through my phone and decided that me complimenting a female friend was equivalent to cheating on her. I knew she had a tendency to look through my phone, but I didn't expect her to exhibit actual controlling behaviours for some reason.
The third verse mentions Will Toledo, and my favourite song from Car Seat Headrest, his band. In an earlier version of this bit, it ended at "he did it twice", and I was going to leave it up to interpretation what song I was referring to. I had the idea that Deadlines (Hostile) and Deadlines (Thoughtful) made one set of "twice"s, and Drunk Drivers/Killer Whales and Drunk Drivers/Killer Whales (Single Version) made another. I liked the idea of leaving it open to interpretation, but it occurred to me that, since Deadlines also released as a totally separate song outside of streaming services, it wouldn't be accurate to say that was only a "twice". I needed to add another line anyway, so I did away with the obfuscation and used, "well, three times / four, if you count Wifi, or 99" to finish the rhyme, and to flex my elite ball knowledge of Deadlines demos.
The choruses, as well as the end of the 3rd verse, are about a particular manipulation she employed in the conclusion of our relationship. I won't go into details, for your own sake, but it was an event that caused me to lose a large part of who I am, and changed my outlook on relationships in general.

—Robin
